Every missed call is a missed opportunity. But hiring a full-time receptionist costs $35,000 or more per year. Traditional answering services charge $1-$2 per call and still put callers on hold.

AI phone answering services offer a third option. Instant pickup, intelligent conversation, 24/7 availability, and a fraction of the cost. But which one should you choose?

We compared the most popular options so you do not have to.

## What Makes a Good AI Phone Answering Service?

Before diving into specific services, here is what separates a great AI answering service from a mediocre one.

### Conversation Quality

The AI should sound natural, handle interruptions gracefully, and manage real conversations. Not just scripted decision trees. Callers should feel like they are talking to a helpful, competent person.

### Business Customization

A generic answering service that cannot tell callers about your specific hours, services, or pricing is nearly useless. The best services let you deeply customize the AI's knowledge and personality.

### Integration with Your Tools

The AI should connect to your calendar for scheduling, your CRM for lead tracking, and your phone system for seamless call handling. Without integrations, you are just adding another disconnected tool.

### Call Routing and Escalation

Not every call should be handled entirely by AI. The best services know when to transfer to a live person and do so seamlessly.

### Reporting and Transcripts

You need to know what is happening on your phones. Call recordings, transcripts, analytics, and summaries are essential for monitoring quality and improving over time.

## How to Choose the Right Service for Your Business

### Consider Your Call Volume

**Under 100 calls/month:** A budget-friendly platform like Goodcall or Rosie may be sufficient.

**100-500 calls/month:** A mid-tier solution with better customization and integrations makes sense.

**500+ calls/month:** A custom-built solution pays for itself quickly due to per-call savings and better conversion rates.

### Consider Your Industry

Some industries have specific needs:

**Healthcare/Dental:** HIPAA compliance is non-negotiable. Make sure any service you choose is fully compliant.

**Legal:** Confidentiality requirements and intake complexity favor custom or hybrid solutions.

**Home Services:** Industry-specific platforms like Rosie understand your workflow out of the box.

**General Business:** Custom solutions offer the most flexibility.

### Consider Your Technical Comfort

**Not technical at all:** Go with a managed solution or agency-built system. Someone else handles the complexity.

**Somewhat technical:** Self-service platforms with good onboarding can work well.

**Very technical:** You might consider building your own using platforms like Vapi or Retell (though this requires ongoing maintenance).

## Setting Up Your AI Answering Service

Regardless of which service you choose, the setup process follows a similar pattern.

### Step 1: Document Your Call Flows

Map out what happens when someone calls your business:

- What are the top 10 questions callers ask?
- What information do you need to collect from new callers?
- When should calls be transferred to a live person?
- What hours should the AI handle calls vs. forwarding?
- How should emergencies or urgent calls be handled?

### Step 2: Prepare Your Business Information

Gather the details your AI will need:

- Business hours and location
- Service descriptions and pricing
- Frequently asked questions and answers
- Staff names and roles
- Scheduling availability and rules
- Special instructions for different call types

### Step 3: Configure and Test

Set up the AI with your information and run test calls:

- Call from different numbers
- Ask every question you can think of
- Try to confuse the AI with unexpected requests
- Test the transfer and escalation process
- Have friends or staff call and give honest feedback

### Step 4: Go Live Gradually

Start by forwarding calls to the AI only during off-hours or when lines are busy. Once you are confident in the quality, expand to handling more calls.
