It's 7 AM. You're about to head to a job site. Your phone shows 12 missed calls and 8 text messages from yesterday.

Some are urgent. Some are tire-kickers. Some are customers you quoted weeks ago. You don't have time to sort through them all.

By 9 PM, you've worked a full day, and those messages are still waiting.

This is the contractor's curse: Too busy working to run the business.

## The Contractor Communication Problem

### Leads Come at the Worst Times

Homeowners call when they have problems:
- Morning (you're commuting or starting a job)
- Lunch (you're actually eating)
- Evening (you're exhausted)
- Weekend (you're trying to recover)

Miss the call, and they call the next contractor.

### You Can't Answer While Working

On a roof? Under a sink? Operating machinery?

You literally cannot pick up the phone. And you shouldn't have to risk injury to answer "how much do you charge?"

### Follow-Up Falls Through the Cracks

You gave a quote last Tuesday. Meant to follow up. Got busy. Now it's been two weeks and they hired someone else.

Sound familiar? You're not alone. 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups, but most contractors give up after one or two.

## How AI Solves Contractor Problems

### Instant Lead Response

**Customer text (3:47 PM while you're on a job):**
"Hi, I need a quote for a new deck. About 400 square feet."

**AI (3:47 PM):**
"Thanks for reaching out about your deck project! A 400 sq ft deck typically ranges from $12,000-$20,000 depending on materials and design.

To give you an accurate quote, I have a few questions:
1. What material are you thinking? (Composite, pressure-treated, cedar, etc.)
2. What's the access like to your backyard?
3. Is there an existing deck to remove?

Also, I can schedule a free on-site estimate. I have availability Thursday at 10 AM or Saturday at 9 AM. Would either work?"

While you're focused on your current job, AI is booking your next one.

### Smart Estimate Scheduling

**Customer:** "Saturday at 9 works"

**AI:** "Perfect! You're confirmed for Saturday at 9 AM for a deck estimate. I'll send you:
- A confirmation text/email
- The address we have on file (please confirm)
- What to expect during the estimate visit

The estimate takes about 30-45 minutes. Is there anything specific you'd like to discuss during the visit?"

No phone tag. No back-and-forth. Just confirmed appointments.

### Automatic Quote Follow-Up

After you send a quote:

**Day 2:** "Hi [Name], just checking if you had questions about the deck quote I sent. Happy to explain any line items or discuss options."

**Day 5:** "Following up on your deck project. I know these decisions take time. If budget is a concern, I can suggest some alternatives that might work better."

**Day 10:** "Hi [Name], I have some availability opening up in my schedule. If you're still interested in moving forward with the deck, now would be a great time to get on the calendar. Otherwise, no worries, and feel free to reach out anytime!"

You never forget to follow up again.

### Emergency Triage

**Customer (11 PM):** "WATER IS POURING FROM MY CEILING!"

**AI:** "I understand this is urgent! Water emergencies need quick action. I'm notifying our emergency team now.

While you wait for a callback (within 15 minutes):
1. Find your main water shutoff valve (usually near the water meter)
2. Turn it clockwise to shut off water
3. If the source is from upstairs, there may be a toilet or appliance leak

If you can't find the shutoff or water is near electrical, please call 911.

Someone will call you back shortly."

Real emergencies get escalated. "How much is a new faucet?" can wait until morning.

## Setting Up AI for Your Trade

### Step 1: Define Your Services

List what you offer with:
- Service name
- Typical price range
- Duration/timeline
- Common questions

### Step 2: Create Response Templates

**For common inquiries:**
```
Thanks for reaching out about [SERVICE]!

[SERVICE] typically costs [PRICE RANGE] and takes 
[TIMELINE].

To give you an accurate quote, I need to know:
[QUALIFYING QUESTIONS]

I can also schedule a free estimate at:
[AVAILABLE TIMES]
```

### Step 3: Set Availability Rules

- When can estimates be scheduled?
- How much notice required?
- Which days are off-limits?
- Service area boundaries?

### Step 4: Configure Urgency Detection

Keywords that trigger emergency escalation:
- "Flooding," "leak," "burst"
- "No heat," "no hot water"
- "Sparking," "burning smell"
- "Gas smell," "carbon monoxide"

### Step 5: Build Follow-Up Sequences

**After estimate:**
- Day 1: "Thanks for having me out"
- Day 3: Answer common objections
- Day 7: Limited time offer
- Day 14: "Should I check back later?"

**After completed job:**
- Day 1: Thank you + review request
- Week 2: "Everything still working well?"
- Month 6: Maintenance reminder
- Year 1: Annual check-in

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will customers know they're talking to AI?

Most customers don't notice or care if they get fast, helpful responses. You can disclose or not based on preference.

### Can AI give accurate quotes?

AI gives ranges and schedules proper estimates. Final quotes still come from you after seeing the job.

### What about emergency calls at night?

AI triages emergencies and can escalate to you via text/call for genuine emergencies while handling non-urgent inquiries for morning.

### I'm not tech-savvy. Can I still use this?

Yes. Modern AI platforms are designed for non-technical users. If you can text, you can set up AI.

### How do I handle jobs that need to be seen first?

AI explains that on-site estimates are needed and schedules them. "Pricing varies based on conditions, so I'll schedule a free estimate to give you an accurate quote."
